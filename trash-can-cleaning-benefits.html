Trash Can Cleaning: Why It's Actually Worth It

By Ultra View · June 20, 2026 · 4 min read

Nobody thinks about their garbage can until they lift the lid on a hot July day and instantly regret it. But that smell is a symptom of something you don't want sitting by your garage: a film of bacteria and decaying gunk coating the inside of the bin. Here's what a professional trash can cleaning does, and why it's one of the easiest add-ons homeowners around here book.

The short version: A professional bin cleaning removes the odor at its source and eliminates up to 99.9% of bacteria, viruses and fungi — the E. coli, salmonella and listeria that build up in your trash and recycling cans.

What's actually living in your bin

Trash and recycling bins are a perfect home for bacteria. Leaked juices, food scraps, and moisture create exactly the environment germs love. Tests of household bins routinely turn up E. coli, salmonella, and listeria — the same bacteria you carefully avoid in your kitchen — growing a few feet from your garage door.

The smell won't fix itself

Rinsing with a hose just spreads it around. The odor comes from bacteria and decaying organic matter bonded to the plastic, and it keeps regenerating until that layer is actually removed. A real deep-clean attacks the source, so the bin comes back genuinely fresh instead of masked with a spray.

Dirty bins invite pests

That food residue and smell is a dinner bell for exactly the visitors you don't want: flies, maggots, rodents, and the raccoons that tip your can over at 2 a.m. Keeping bins clean removes the food source that draws them in — a small thing that saves a lot of mess.

How we clean them

We deep-clean the inside and out, scrubbing away the built-up grime, then deodorize and sanitize so the bin is fresh and germ-free — not just rinsed. It's quick, and it pairs perfectly with a window cleaning visit so you knock out both in one stop.

How often is worth it

Once or twice a year keeps most household bins in good shape; monthly or quarterly is popular for families, homes with pets, or anyone whose cans live in the garage. Ask us about one-time cleans and recurring plans when you book your quote — we clean cans right alongside our window service across La Grange, Western Springs and Hinsdale.

Frequently asked questions

Is professional trash can cleaning worth it?

If your bins smell, attract pests, or live in the garage, yes. A professional clean removes up to 99.9% of bacteria and eliminates odor at its source, which a hose rinse can't do.

How often should I get my trash cans cleaned?

Once or twice a year works for most homes; monthly or quarterly is popular for families, pet owners, or bins kept in the garage.

Do you clean recycling bins too?

Yes — we clean both garbage and recycling bins, deep-cleaning, deodorizing and sanitizing inside and out.
